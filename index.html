Research Lab

Prefiguration Lab

We study how activism, digital rights, and emerging technologies shape more sustainable futures.

Our work connects activists, communities, and policymakers to understand how change is imagined — and prefigured — in the present. We focus on digital rights activism, AI governance, and grassroots movements that experiment with more just ways of organizing.

Digital rights AI governance Sustainable policymaking Activism

Current focus

  • Digital rights activism and data protection
  • AI governance and public accountability
  • Cycling activism and urban mobility
  • Storytelling and public engagement through Homo Digitalis

Podcast

Homo Digitalis

Homo Digitalis is the podcast of the Prefiguration Lab. We explore how digital technologies, platforms, and data practices reshape activism, work, and everyday life.

Each episode brings together scholars, activists, and practitioners to discuss digital rights, AI governance, and the politics of technology. The podcast is designed as a space where research and activism meet, making complex debates accessible to a wider public.

You can listen directly below or follow the show on Spotify and other podcast platforms.

Digital Rights Activism – Introduction

An introduction to digital rights activism as a form of prefigurative politics, and how campaigns around data and algorithms shape public debate.

GDPR and Everyday Data Justice

A starter episode on the GDPR, exploring how data protection rules matter for everyday life and for broader struggles around digital rights and accountability.
